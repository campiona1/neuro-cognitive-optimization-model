# neuro-cognitive-optimization-model
Challenging Mass Coaching: Developing a Micro-Batch (≤12) STEM Model for Optimized Neuro-Cognitive Learning. Housing the open-source protocol files, variable codebooks, and federated data structures for our global multi-site CRCT research.
## 📝 Core Research Protocol & Methodology

### 1. Introduction & Theoretical Framework
The dominant mass-coaching model treats secondary STEM cohorts as large statistical groups, creating an Efficiency Gap between instructional delivery and durable learning. Cognitive Load Theory and the Zone of Proximal Development (ZPD) suggest that limited teacher attention and high environmental noise increase extraneous cognitive load, reducing encoding into long-term memory. We propose the Small Batch Size Model (≤12 students) to optimize teacher-student interaction loops and environmental design.

### 2. Testable Hypotheses
* **Primary Hypothesis:** Classes organized in small batches (≤12 students) will show greater normalized gains on standardized STEM concept tests from pretest to immediate posttest and at 3-month follow-up, compared to standard large batches (≥25 students), controlling for baseline achievement and clustering.
* **Secondary Hypotheses:** * Interaction frequency (teacher-student turns per student per hour) will be higher in small batches and will mediate learning gains.
  * Small batches will show lower measured extraneous cognitive load and reduced reports of learned helplessness.

### 3. Literature Synthesis Summary
Cognitive bandwidth and working memory limits constrain simultaneous processing of academic and social information, supporting interventions that reduce extraneous load. ZPD and scaffolding theory emphasize tailored feedback that is hard to scale in large classes. While meta-analyses of class size show variable effects, STEM subjects exhibit greater sensitivity to class-size changes due to their reliance on complex, cumulative reasoning. 

### 4. Proposed Primary CRCT Design
* **Unit of Randomization:** Class/coaching-center cluster.
* **Arms:** Small-batch intervention (≤12) vs. Standard-batch control (≥25–30).
* **Participants:** Secondary STEM students (grades 9–12 equivalent) attending participating centers; stratified by site type, baseline achievement, and urban/rural location.
* **Analysis Plan:** Multilevel mixed-effects models (students nested in clusters), intention-to-treat analysis, and mediation analysis for interaction frequency.

### 5. Operational Small-Batch Protocol
* **Cohort Size:** Maximum 12 students.
* **Teacher Ratio & Training:** One instructor per cohort; 8-hour standardized training module on active questioning, wait-time management (<5s target), immediate feedback strategies, and the Neuro-Map method for tracking conceptual gaps.
* **Interaction Targets:** Minimum 6–8 turns/student/hour.
* **Classroom Architecture:** Minimize peripheral sensory stimuli (Design for Focus Environment), structured seating, and signaling protocols for attention resets.
